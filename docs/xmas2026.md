# Dominican Republic — Villa Options

The family's current front-runner. Two gated, upscale areas each do exactly what
we want: one big staffed house with a pool and a calm beach, everyone under one
roof, in a very safe setting. Both have deep 7-night villa inventory for our
dates at prices below the Jamaica and Tulum tiers — and the DR sidesteps the
Mexico safety worry while giving us the gated-community options we like.

**Our search:** Dec 22–29, 2026 (7 nights) · 8 adults + 2 infants (our 10
travelers) · 5+ bedrooms. Airbnb totals include fees and were quoted 2026-07-18;
**holiday listings reprice, so treat these as ballpark and re-quote before booking.**

## At a glance

| | Casa de Campo (La Romana) | Cap Cana (Punta Cana) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Getting there | Fly **PUJ**, ~1 hr private transfer (LRM is 10 min away but has no Chicago nonstop) | Fly **PUJ**, 15–20 min — nonstop from **both** NYC and Chicago |
| Beach for little kids | **Minitas** — sheltered bay, flat calm water, ideal for toddlers | **Juanillo** — long powdery beach, calm aquamarine; rated the finer beach |
| The setup | Gated resort of ~1,700 *private villas*: 24/7 security, golf carts, marina, Altos de Chavón — a house *with* resort amenities around it | Gated luxury community of private villas + beach clubs, golf, marina |
| Staff | Villas commonly include housekeeping, often a cook | Varies by villa — confirm per listing |
| 5BR villa, 7 nights | ~\$11.5K–13K value tier; staffed / marina tiers \$20–38K | ~\$5.3K–12K value tier; staffed / Luxe \$24–29K |

**The trade-off:** Cap Cana wins on flights (PUJ is 15 minutes away, nonstop from
both cities) and arguably on the beach. Casa de Campo wins on the
house-inside-a-gated-resort character and the staffed-villa norm, but sits about
an hour from the airport.

## Casa de Campo (La Romana)

A gated resort spanning ~7,000 acres with roughly 1,700 privately owned villas,
24/7 security, golf carts as the way to get around, calm Minitas Beach, a marina,
Altos de Chavón (replica of a 16th-century Mediterranean village), and its own restaurants. Villas here commonly include
housekeeping and often a cook.

## Cap Cana (Punta Cana)

A gated luxury community 15–20 minutes from PUJ: private villas across golf, marina,
and beach neighborhoods, plus the Juanillo and Api beach clubs, Scape Park, and
24/7 security. Juanillo is arguably the best beach of any of our finalists.
Staffing varies more listing-to-listing here — confirm per villa. Airbnb showed
~115 five-bedroom-plus villas for our exact dates, all quoting a full 7 nights.

## Beyond Airbnb

Both areas have a big villa-agency market that often guarantees in-resort
location and bundles staff, golf cart, and concierge — worth a direct cross-shop:

- [Casa de Campo official rentals](https://www.casadecampo.com.do/villas/) — book direct, in-resort, staff packages
- [The Top Villas — Casa de Campo](https://www.thetopvillas.com/destination/casa-de-campo)
- [Rental Escapes](https://www.rentalescapes.com/) — Casa de Campo & Cap Cana luxe tier
- [Isle Blue — Dominican Republic](https://isleblue.co/dominican-republic/villas)
- **VRBO** — same-style search as Airbnb; overlapping owners, different fees
- For **Cap Cana**, look at the AILA, Las Palmas, and Marina neighborhoods —
  several villas are managed by the Cap Cana rental desk

## How the DR compares to the other finalists

- **vs. Jamaica villas:** same staffed-house model, lower 7-night totals (value
  tier \$11–13K vs. Jamaica \$14–17K+ for 4BR) and — the family's finding —
  Jamaica had very little left for our dates.
- **vs. Riviera Maya:** comparable or cheaper, with Minitas/Juanillo matching
  Soliman Bay's calm water — and it clears the Mexico safety concern the family
  raised.
- **vs. Puerto Rico:** cheaper than the \$22K Sabana quote and offers the same
  gated-community structure — but PR keeps the no-passport, US-logistics edge.
